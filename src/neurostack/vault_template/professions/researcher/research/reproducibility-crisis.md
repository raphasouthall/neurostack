# Reproducibility Crisis

The reproducibility crisis refers to the finding that a substantial proportion of published research results cannot be independently replicated, with failure rates ranging from 50-90% depending on the field.

## Key Drivers

- **P-hacking**: Flexible analysis choices that inflate false-positive rates (Simmons et al., 2011)
- **Publication bias**: Journals preferentially publish positive results, creating a distorted literature
- **Small samples**: Underpowered studies produce noisy effect estimates that don't replicate
- **HARKing**: Hypothesising After Results are Known — presenting exploratory findings as confirmatory

## Structural Fixes

- **Pre-registration** — commit to hypotheses and analysis plan before data collection (OSF, AsPredicted)
- **Registered Reports** — peer review before results exist, decoupling acceptance from outcome
- **Open data/code** — enable computational reproducibility
- **Multi-site replication** — large-scale coordinated replications (e.g., Many Labs)
