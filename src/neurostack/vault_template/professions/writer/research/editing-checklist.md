# Editing Checklist

Editing is not one task. It is a series of distinct passes, each with a different lens. Trying to fix everything at once leads to fixing nothing well. Work from macro to micro.

## Pass 1 — Structural Edit

- Does the story answer its central dramatic question?
- Does every scene advance plot, reveal character, or both?
- Are there scenes that can be cut without the reader noticing? Cut them
- Does the midpoint genuinely raise stakes or shift direction?
- Is the climax earned by everything that came before?
- Check against [[story-structure-frameworks]] — does the shape feel right?

## Pass 2 — Character & Arc

- Does each POV character want something in every scene?
- Are character decisions driving the plot, or is the plot dragging characters?
- Is the protagonist's internal arc (lie → truth) tracked across the story?
- Do secondary characters have their own agendas, not just service the protagonist?
- Read all dialogue for one character in sequence — is the voice consistent?

## Pass 3 — Scene-Level

- Does each scene have a clear entry point and turning point?
- Is the emotional state different at the end of the scene than the beginning?
- Are scene transitions doing work (contrast, momentum, juxtaposition)?
- Is exposition woven into action, or dumped in blocks?

## Pass 4 — Prose & Style

- Read aloud — mark every place you stumble or run out of breath
- Cut adverbs that duplicate what the verb already implies
- Replace vague nouns with specific ones (building → bungalow, car → station wagon)
- Check for [[show-dont-tell]] violations at emotional beats
- Vary sentence length — short sentences for tension, longer for flow
- Kill filter words: "she felt", "he noticed", "they realised" — just render the sensation

## Pass 5 — Line Edit & Proofread

- Consistency: names, timelines, physical details, established rules
- Repeated words within the same paragraph or page
- Dialogue tags — "said" is invisible, everything else should earn its place
- Punctuation, spelling, formatting

## The Cardinal Rule of Revision

Let the draft rest between passes. The minimum gap for useful self-editing is 48 hours. You cannot edit what you remember writing — you can only edit what you read.
