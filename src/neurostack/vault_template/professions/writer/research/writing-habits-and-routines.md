# Writing Habits and Routines

Talent is common. Finishing is rare. The difference between writers who publish and writers who don't is almost never quality — it is consistency. A sustainable writing practice matters more than any single technique.

## Core Principles

- **Quantity produces quality** — you cannot edit a blank page. Bad drafts become good stories; no drafts become nothing
- **Schedule over inspiration** — treat writing time as an appointment, not a mood-dependent activity
- **Protect the first hour** — creative work degrades with decision fatigue. Write before email, before meetings, before consuming other people's words
- **Separate drafting from editing** — they use different cognitive modes. Switching between them in one session destroys momentum

## Practical Routines

### Daily Minimum

Set a minimum so low it feels embarrassing — 200 words, 15 minutes. The goal is to never break the chain. On good days you will exceed it. On bad days you still wrote.

### Session Structure

1. **Re-read last paragraph** — reconnect with the voice and momentum
2. **Draft forward** — no going back, no editing, no research. Leave [BRACKETS] for things to look up later
3. **End mid-sentence** — Hemingway's trick. Stopping at a point where you know what comes next makes starting tomorrow effortless
4. **Log the session** — word count, time, energy level, what worked. Patterns emerge over weeks

### Managing Resistance

- **The two-minute rule**: Commit to just opening the document. Most resistance dissolves once you start
- **Boredom is a signal**: If you are bored writing a scene, the reader will be bored reading it. Cut or restructure
- **Perfectionism is procrastination in a suit**: The inner critic has no place in a first draft. Give it a job during [[editing-checklist|editing]]

## Word Count Benchmarks

These are descriptive, not prescriptive. They help calibrate expectations:

- **Short story**: 1,000-7,500 words
- **Novelette**: 7,500-17,500 words
- **Novella**: 17,500-40,000 words
- **Novel**: 70,000-100,000 words (genre-dependent)
- **Sustainable daily output**: 500-2,000 words for most writers
